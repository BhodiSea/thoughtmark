# Phase 8 — Interop (C2PA/CAWG + PROV-O export) + Standardization

> Project the frozen native schema *outward* — as lossy, never-re-hashed interop views — and claim the open-standard white-space, without ever touching the byte-format train. **Source:** [roadmap.md §6 Phase 5](../research/roadmap.md), [roadmap.md §5 prior art / FTO + Recommendations 4 & 6](../research/roadmap.md), [roadmap.md Thresholds](../research/roadmap.md); [architecture.md §9 `thoughtmark-c2pa`](../research/architecture.md), [§5.10 PROV-O export](../research/architecture.md), [§14.1 / §14.5 `export` verb](../research/architecture.md), [§16 canon-evolution endgame](../research/architecture.md), [§2.2 P6 export firewall](../research/architecture.md); [quality-foundations.md Domain 8](../research/quality-foundations.md).

This is the architecture's [§17 "Phase 5 — interop + redaction hardening + standardization"](../research/architecture.md) and the roadmap's [§6 Phase 5](../research/roadmap.md). It renumbers to roadmap-file Phase 8 because it depends on the post-1.0 redaction work (Phase 6) and the reference app (Phase 5) that the research docs fold together. Nothing here mutates a hashed byte; the entire phase lives *downstream* of the [§16](../research/architecture.md) freeze gates.

## Objective

Ship the outward-facing surface of `thoughtmark` so that a verified trail can be (a) projected to **W3C PROV-O** JSON-LD and **C2PA v2.3+ text-manifest / CAWG identity assertions** as one-way interop views, (b) hardened against redaction/erasure attack in production, and (c) **published as an Internet-Draft / community spec** that claims the unclaimed niche — a signed, structured, per-turn-attributed multi-turn human–AI reasoning trail. The load-bearing constraint is the [P6 export firewall](../research/architecture.md): every projection is **lossy, one-way, and NEVER re-hashed**, which is what isolates C2PA/CAWG/PROV-O spec churn and RDF-dataset-canonicalization nondeterminism from the byte-format train frozen at the end of [Phase 2](phase-2-log-signing.md) and [Phase 3](phase-3-verify-apifreeze.md). The phase also stands up the **canon-evolution endgame**: the machinery to land a genuinely breaking `tm-jcs-2` while every 1.x verifier keeps working forever.

## Deliverables

- **`thoughtmark-c2pa` — the C2PA v2.3+ / CAWG export adapter** ([arch §9](../research/architecture.md), [§15.5](../research/architecture.md), crate sits at [arch §3.2 / §3.3](../research/architecture.md) depending only on `thoughtmark-core`, never depended-on by it). Produces a C2PA **external/sidecar** manifest with a **DataHash hard binding** over the *final-artifact* bytes (the rendered PDF/answer, not the trail leaves), a **CAWG (Creator Assertions Working Group, now in DIF) Identity Assertion** carrying the human DID/VC + AI model id, and a plain-text Content Credentials summary, shipped alongside the `.tmbundle` produced in [Phase 5](phase-5-reference-app.md). Per [Recommendation 4](../research/roadmap.md), conversation provenance is **NOT** forced into C2PA's single-asset model — C2PA is a downstream view of the *final artifact*, not a re-encoding of the turn DAG.
- **The `export(ProvO | C2pa)` verbs** ([arch §14.1 / §14.5](../research/architecture.md)). The Rust signature is `pub fn export(statement: &serde_json::Value, fmt: ExportFormat) -> Result<serde_json::Value>` with `pub enum ExportFormat { ProvO, C2pa }`; the TS mirror is `export_(statement, fmt)` because **`export` is a reserved word in TS** ([arch §14.6](../research/architecture.md) — documented rename, `ExportFormat = "prov-o" | "c2pa"`). Both verbs are **core, pure** but their output is **lossy, one-way, and NEVER canonicalized or fed back into `hash()`** ([arch §14.1 contract](../research/architecture.md), [P6](../research/architecture.md)). This firewall is what contains C2PA/CAWG/PROV-O spec churn and RDF-canonicalization nondeterminism away from the frozen byte-format train.
- **PROV-O projection** ([arch §5.10](../research/architecture.md), signature `pub fn export_prov(trail, turns, manifests) -> serde_json::Value`). Mapping: `Turn`/blob → `prov:Entity`; `Action`/`LedgerEntry` → `prov:Activity` (typed by the verb); `Participant` → `prov:Agent` with the AI participant subtyped **`tm:AIAgent`**. Relations: entity `prov:wasGeneratedBy` its create-Activity; `prov:wasAttributedTo` its `attributed_to`; a child turn `prov:wasDerivedFrom` each parent (with the `supersedes` edge → `tm:supersedes`); an AI Activity `prov:used` its `RunManifest` Entity. It is **derived on demand from the native schema (the sole oracle), never stored, never hashed** — JSON-LD would require RDF Dataset Canonicalization, a divergence-prone algorithm distinct from our JCS ([I2](../research/architecture.md)), so hashing it would forfeit byte-identity ([I1](../research/architecture.md)).
- **Redaction hardening — productionize the [Phase 6](phase-6-identity-redaction.md) schemes** ([arch §9.2](../research/architecture.md)). Mature the **redactable-Merkle subtree pruning** (replace a pruned leaf with its known `TreeHash`; root and all anchored roots / consistency proofs stay valid; RFC 6962 `0x00`/`0x01` separation prevents leaf-as-node confusion) and **crypto-shredding** (XChaCha20-Poly1305 with an injected 24-byte nonce; commit the ciphertext's salted hash; erase by deleting the per-subject key from the `ShredKeyStore`). Add the implementation-risk ADR documenting that v1 stays **sign-over-the-salted-commitment** ([ADR-0011](../research/architecture.md)) — true homomorphic / Merkle-RSS redactable signatures remain deferred research because they are unaudited relative to `ed25519-dalek` ([I6](../research/architecture.md)). Broaden the **negative / erasure vector** corpus around **remainder verification**: `verify(entry) == verify(redact(entry, policy))` must hold for every proof over non-redacted parts, and `redact` must `Err` ([`REDACT_TARGET_NOT_FOUND`](../research/architecture.md)) on a field that was not committed disclosable at capture.
- **Standardization — `SPEC.md` → Internet-Draft** ([qf Domain 8](../research/quality-foundations.md), [roadmap §6 P5](../research/roadmap.md)). Promote the BCP-14 normative `SPEC.md` (stable requirement IDs, traceable to `spec/vectors/`) into an I-D authored in **`kramdown-rfc`**, and submit the reasoning-trail provenance schema as an Internet-Draft / community spec to **claim the standardization white-space**: per [roadmap §4(e)/§5](../research/roadmap.md), no existing open standard (C2PA, IPTC, CAWG, HDP, VAP, IPP) models a signed multi-turn human–AI dialogue with per-turn attribution. The I-D normatively references the frozen format-identifier values from [arch §14.2](../research/architecture.md) (`canon_version="tm-jcs-1"`, `predicateType=…/Provenance/v1`, DSSE `payloadType`, bundle media type) so the spec text and the wire bytes cannot drift.
- **FTO review against the Microsoft authorship-attribution patents** ([roadmap §5](../research/roadmap.md), [Recommendation 6](../research/roadmap.md)) — **US 12,061,902; 12,190,106; 12,462,318; 12,493,775; 12,517,723** — completed **before any commercial layer**. The chosen mitigations, faithfully: keep attribution **participant-ATTESTED** (PROV/DID-based `LedgerEntry` records — `attributed_to`, `Action`, `ApprovalScope`), **never auto-detected authorship tokens** ([arch §19 "attribution gaming"](../research/architecture.md)); and rely on the **Apache-2.0 patent grant + defensive-termination clause** ([roadmap §3](../research/roadmap.md), this is exactly why Apache-2.0 was chosen over MIT/BSD). This is FTO diligence, **not legal advice** — see Risks.
- **The canon-evolution endgame** ([arch §16](../research/architecture.md)). Stand up the machinery (not necessarily a release) so a genuinely breaking canon change can land cleanly: it ships as **`tm-jcs-2` + `Provenance/v2` + `vectors 2.0.0`** simultaneously, the old `canon_v1` module is **frozen and never deleted** (deprecate-and-warn at most), and **1.x verifiers keep working forever** because the verifier dispatches on the `canon_version` bound inside the preimage ([arch §4.5](../research/architecture.md), [P5](../research/architecture.md)). An unknown version **fails closed** (`UnknownCanonVersion`, a permanent negative vector) — never a best-effort recompute, which under guessed rules could forge a PASS.

## Quality gates (must be green)

This phase newly **activates** (the gate net itself was stood up in [Phase 0](phase-0-foundations.md) against stubs; see the [activation rule](../research/quality-foundations.md)):

- **Export-firewall assertion** ([qf Domain 4](../research/quality-foundations.md); [arch §14.7 / P6](../research/architecture.md)) — a test **and** a lint that the output of `export`/`export_`/`export_prov` is **never** fed back into `hash()`/`canonicalize()`. This is the structural enforcement of the byte-format isolation; treat a violation as a security-class failure.
- **Export vectors** ([qf Domain 4 / bootstrap row 10 & 20](../research/quality-foundations.md)) — `spec/vectors/export/{prov-o,c2pa}/**` fixtures: a known native `Statement`/`Trail` → its expected PROV-O JSON-LD and C2PA sidecar manifest, run from both the Rust and WASM/TS executors. Because these views are **not** on the byte-identity path, the equality is *structural* (the PROV-O graph / C2PA manifest shape) rather than the strict byte-parity used on the hashed path — exactly so RDF-canonicalization nondeterminism cannot flow into the conformance gate.
- **`CITATION.cff` + GitHub↔Zenodo DOI** ([qf Domain 8 `[REC]`](../research/quality-foundations.md)) — the research artifact becomes citeable with a stable DOI; wire the Zenodo release integration to the tagged release.
- **`release-train-consistency` for any `tm-jcs-2`** ([arch §16 Format-identifiers train](../research/architecture.md)) — the CI check that **refuses a byte mutation unless a new format-identifier value is introduced**; activated/exercised here so the endgame path (a new `canon_version` + MAJOR corpus 2.0.0 + add-`canon_v2`-never-mutate-`canon_v1`) is mechanically enforced rather than relying on review.
- **Negative / erasure corpus broadening** ([qf bootstrap row 20](../research/quality-foundations.md), [arch §16 permanent negative vectors](../research/architecture.md)) — the remainder-verification property plus `UnknownCanonVersion` / malformed-redaction refusals are permanent and additive-only; removing one or flipping a refusal to an acceptance is a MAJOR (security) event.
- **A fuzz target per new parser** ([activation rule](../research/quality-foundations.md), [arch ADR-0006 `fuzz/` outside the workspace](../research/architecture.md)) — if any export adapter or hardened-redaction step introduces a new untrusted-input parser, it ships with a `cargo-fuzz` target. (Export *emits* rather than parses, so the new fuzz surface is primarily on any C2PA/CAWG ingest used for round-trip tests.)

## Exit / freeze gate

- The reasoning-trail provenance schema is **submitted as an Internet-Draft** (authored in `kramdown-rfc`, normatively referencing the [arch §14.2](../research/architecture.md) format-identifier values), and the white-space is publicly claimed.
- **C2PA/CAWG + PROV-O export round-trips** (native → view, and view rendered/inspected) **without touching the byte-format train** — the export-firewall test/lint is green and no export output ever reaches `hash()`.
- **`CITATION.cff` + Zenodo DOI** published with the release.
- Per [arch §17](../research/architecture.md): **export churn is contained (never re-hashed, P6)**, and the canon-evolution endgame is demonstrably available — any breaking canon change would land as `tm-jcs-2` + `Provenance/v2` + `vectors 2.0.0` **while 1.x keeps verifying**. This phase does **not** itself break canon; it proves the system *could* without orphaning a single retained artifact.

## Risks & open implementation-gaps

- **C2PA versioning moves fast and its "text support" has no conversation semantics** ([roadmap Caveats](../research/roadmap.md), [§5](../research/roadmap.md)). v2.3 text support landed January 2026 and **v2.4 followed**; the v2.3+ "text" mechanism is a **steganographic Unicode-variation-selector embedding** of a media-style manifest into a single text blob (C2PA Appendix A.7/A.8, authored by Encypher) — it treats a text blob as one asset and carries **no turn/dialogue model**. **Re-verify the current C2PA spec immediately before building the adapter.** This is precisely why the conversation schema is native and C2PA is only a *final-artifact* export view ([Recommendation 4](../research/roadmap.md)).
- **PIVOT threshold — if C2PA ships a real conversation/turn assertion** (beyond the v2.3/v2.4 text blobs) ([roadmap Thresholds](../research/roadmap.md)): pivot from "own the schema" to "extend C2PA." The export adapter would then become a first-class bridge rather than a lossy downstream view.
- **PRIORITIZE threshold — if a competing IETF draft (an HDP/VAP successor) adopts conversational reasoning-trail provenance** ([roadmap Thresholds](../research/roadmap.md)): prioritize this phase's standardization work **immediately** to avoid being preempted in the white-space.
- **HDP / VAP / IPP are individual IETF drafts (work-in-progress), not adopted standards** ([roadmap Caveats / §5](../research/roadmap.md)) — `draft-helixar-hdp-agentic-delegation-00`, `draft-kamimura-vap-framework-00`, `draft-haberkamp-ipp-00`, all <6 months old and addressing delegation / decision-audit rather than conversation. **Cite only as work-in-progress** in the I-D, never as prior-art standards.
- **The Microsoft patent claims are broad** ([roadmap Caveats / §5 / Recommendation 6](../research/roadmap.md)) — the issued authorship-token patents explicitly contemplate publishing tokens to "a blockchain, distributed ledger, Merkle tree." This document flags FTO risk and records the mitigations (participant-attested ledger entries, Apache-2.0 patent grant + defensive termination); it is **not legal advice — get counsel** before any commercial layer.
- **RDF-canonicalization nondeterminism** ([arch §5.10 / P6](../research/architecture.md)) — JSON-LD / PROV-O requires RDF Dataset Canonicalization, an algorithm distinct from and divergence-prone relative to our JCS. The structural mitigation is total: PROV-O is **derived on demand, never stored, never hashed**, and the export-firewall gate enforces it.
- **The `tm-jcs-2` endgame is machinery, not a planned break** ([arch §16](../research/architecture.md)) — this phase ensures the release-train-consistency check, the frozen-`canon_v1` module discipline, and the fail-closed-on-unknown-version behavior are all in place, so that *if* a breaking canon change ever becomes necessary it cannot orphan a retained 2026 artifact in 2030.
